Modifying alphabetizing

Use the Sort panel (Tools menu) to specify alphabetizing rules.

Settings in the Text tab specify the alphabetizing principles to be used.

When you use Word-By-Word or Letter-by-Letter alphabetizing, you can modify the basic alphabetizing rules with settings in the Exceptions box:

Ignore -/

Check this to make Cindex ignore hyphen and slash in word-by-word alphabetizing (they are always ignored in letter-by-letter alphabetizing). In word-by-word alphabetizing Cindex normally treats the hyphen and slash as spaces unless these are the only characters that distinguish entries.

Ignore .,:;

Check this to make Cindex ignore .,:; in letter-by-letter alphabetizing. In standard letter-by-letter alphabetizing, punctuation following a word or phrase has a higher precedence than any letter.

Evaluate Numbers

Check this to make Cindex evaluate numbers that it would normally ignore.

Ignore Parenthetical Phrases

When this is checked, Cindex ignores phrases or sentences in parentheses, unless these are the only text that distinguishes headings.

Ignore Parenthetical Endings

Check this to make Cindex ignore all parenthetical endings to words when alphabetizing entries. Cindex always ignores phrases or sentences in parentheses, unless the phrase or sentence in parentheses is the only text that distinguishes headings.

None of the above settings apply when you are using Simple alphabetizing.

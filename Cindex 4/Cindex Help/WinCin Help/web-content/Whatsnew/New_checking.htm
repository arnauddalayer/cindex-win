New features: checking and searching among entries

Comprehensive index check

Cindex provides a new Check Index… command in the Tools menu. This supersedes and greatly extends the capabilities of the former Check References… command, and the Find Orphaned Subheadings option of the Reconcile Headings… command.

The available checks fall into three broad groups covering:

  1. Errors within entries, including misused special characters, misplaced or questionable punctuation, letter case errors.
  2. Inconsistencies across headings, such as letter case, style or punctuation, plurals; also orphaned subheadings.
  3. Errors in references, such as missing or otherwise incorrect cross-references; overlapping or excessive numbers of page references.

Search and replace
When you use Find… or Replace… to search among records made or modified on particular date(s), Cindex now understands dates entered in a much wider variety of formats. It will also alert you immediately if it cannot understand a date you provide.

You can now search for text or records that do not carry specified style or font attributes.

Cindex provides a rich set of ready-made patterns for you to use with Find… and Replace… (via a contextual menu displayed when you right-click in the find box). The set of patterns is now extended to include text in quotes or parentheses (but excluding the quotes or parentheses), and proper names.

Entering cross-references in records

You can enter cross-references as subheadings (rather than in the locator field), but this is not recommended, because Cindex does not provide its full-range of services for managing such references. You must set preferences before Cindex will recognize cross-references entered as subheadings.

Remember these points when entering cross-references in records:

  • Cindex recognizes a cross-reference by identifying its introductory phrase as one of the following: See, See also, See under, or See also under. If your index uses cross-references that begin with some other kind of introductory word or phrase, you must designate that explicitly.
  • When you type a cross-reference, you need not apply a style to the introductory phrase. When Cindex displays formatted entries it can apply different designated styles automatically to the introductory phrase and to the body of a cross-reference.
  • When enter multiple cross-references in a single record you should always separate successive ones with the designated separating character, usually a semicolon.
  • When you put several cross-references in one locator field, you need not place them in alphabetical order. Cindex will sort them when it displays the formatted entries.

Formatting page ranges

Use the Page References panel (Document menu) to specify how ranges of page references should be formatted.

When you enter references in records you must use a single character (typically a hyphen, or minus sign) to denote a connected range of pages such as 55–67. When Cindex displays formatted entries, it translates the connecting character to another character or sequence of characters you specify. References that appear in records as 55-67 can therefore be displayed as 55–67, or 55 to 67, or 55 through 57.

Cindex can conflate references to consecutive pages, so that a series of references entered in records as

34, 33, 35, 37, 39, 40-42, 41-43

can appear as

33-35, 37, 39-43

Cindex can abbreviate the upper part of an inclusive range of page numbers, so that a range entered in a record as 920-923 might be displayed as 920-23.

Set Connecting Text to the character or sequence of characters that Cindex will display in formatted entries instead of the single character you have used in a record to denote an inclusive range. By default Cindex substitutes an en-dash for the hyphen used in records.

To conflate a series of separate references to successive pages into a single inclusive range (e.g., 55, 56, 57 becomes 55-57) choose from the Conflate Beyond drop-down list the number of successive references Cindex must encounter before conflating them.

To abbreviate the upper element of an inclusive range of page numbers (e.g., to show 105-107 as 105-7) choose a method from the Abbreviation Rule dropdown list. Abbreviation methods in the list are ordered least to most aggressive. Choose Chicago to use rules recommended by The Chicago Manual of Style or Hart to use the method recommended by Hart's Rules. Choose Full to abbreviate the second number to the smallest possible number of digits.

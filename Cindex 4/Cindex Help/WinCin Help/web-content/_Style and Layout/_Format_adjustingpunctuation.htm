Adjusting punctuation

When displaying an index in fully formatted view, Cindex punctuates entries automatically, for example, placing a comma and space immediately after the text of an entry, before the references. Cindex can make automatic adjustments to punctuation so that, for example, the comma is brought inside any closing quotation mark at the end of the text.

To specify whether Cindex should adjust punctuation of entries, choose Style & Layout... from the Document menu. The relevant settings are under Adjust Punctuation.

To make Cindex adjust the punctuation so that a period or comma is automatically brought inside a closing quotation mark, check Around Quotation Marks.

To make Cindex extend a style such as italics or boldface over trailing punctuation, check Around Styled Text. When this is checked, Cindex will also automatically italicize any opening and closing parentheses around a phrase that is fully italicized.

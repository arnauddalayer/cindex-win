Making groups

You can define sets ("groups") of records that you can work with as though they were the only records in the index. This provides a convenient method for you to manage the entries in large indexes.

You can make a group from selected records in the main index window, or from the results of a search using Find (Edit menu).

  • To make a group from records you have selected in the main window, choose New Group from the File menu (also available through the contextual menu Cindex displays when you control-click or right-click in the main window).
  • To make a group from records that contain specified text or have particular attributes, choose Find… from the Edit menu, set up your search, then click Find All. Cindex will examine all the records that could be displayed in the current view and will form a group from those that meet your search criteria.

Cindex also automatically makes a temporary group when you duplicate a selection of records.

When Cindex makes a group it gives it the name "Temporary Group" and automatically displays it in the main window.

To restore the view of all records, choose All Records from the View menu. To view the group choose Temporary Group from the View menu.

The temporary group survives until you make a new one, or until you save it as a permanent group (see below) or finish your session with Cindex.

You can search among the records in a group, or view the fully formatted entries in it. You can sort a group differently from the main index.

To save a temporary group as a permanent one that you can recall at any time, first make sure it is on view (if necessary, choose Temporary Group from the View menu), then choose Save Group… from the File menu (or the contextual menu you obtain with right-click in the main window). Cindex automatically associates the saved groups with their parent index.

To view records in a saved group, choose the group by name from the Group submenu in the View menu (this item is dimmed if the index contains no saved groups).
